APJ Abdul Kalam

Missile Man of India

Here's a Timeline of Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

  • 1931 - Born in Rameswaram,Madras,Tamil Nadu, India.
  • 1954 - Graduated from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, with a degree in physics.
  • 1958 - Joined Indian Space Research Organisation(ISRO).
  • 1969 - Worked on the SLV (Satellite Launch Vehicle) project, which led to India's first successful satellite launch, Rohini.
  • 1980 - Directed the development of indigenous satellite launch vehicle technology.
  • 1992 - Appointed as the Director of the Defense Research and Development Organization (DRDO).
  • 2002 - Elected as the 11th President of India, serving until 2007. Known as the "People's President" for his approachable and inspiring leadership.
  • 2007 - Returned to a life of education and mentorship.Inspired and motivated countless students and young scientists through lectures and interactions.
  • July 27,2015 - A.P.J. Abdul Kalam passed away at the age of 83, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong.
  • Notable Accomplishments:
    1. A key figure in India's space and defense programs, contributing to the successful launch of several satellites.
    2. Led the development of India's first indigenous satellite launch vehicle, SLV.
    3. Played a pivotal role in India's nuclear weapons program, including the Pokhran-II tests.
    4. Authored several books, including "Wings of Fire," an autobiography.
    5. An inspiration to millions, especially the youth, for his vision, humility, and dedication to science and education.
